NURS Course Schedule

100 Level Courses

NURS 103: Introduction to Professional Nursing. 3 credits.
Introduces students to essential characteristics of professional nursing practice in today’s healthcare system and the interconnected global community. Concepts to be explored include the history and theoretical basis of the nursing profession, the profession as part of a wider healthcare community and delivery system, professional communication, self-reflection, and self-care as means to promote health and wellness of self and others. An introduction to the responsibility of self-directed, life-long learning, and evidence-based practice is included. An emphasis will be placed on the complexity and holism of culturally sensitive nursing care for diverse patient populations. This course provides opportunities for applied learning in the simulation center. Offered by Nursing. Limited to two attempts.
Registration Restrictions:

Enrollment limited to students in a Bach of Science in Nursing degree.

Schedule Type: Laboratory, Lecture
Grading:
This course is graded on the Undergraduate Regular scale.
NURS 113: Population Health Nursing. 3 credits.
Examines the health of groups of individuals and the equitable distribution of health within these groups. Using state and local health data as examples, students are introduced to epidemiology and evidenced-based population health with an emphasis on health promotion and primary and secondary prevention. This course provides opportunities for applied learning in the simulation center.   Offered by Nursing. Limited to two attempts.
